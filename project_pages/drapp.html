Disaster Relief App
❯

Description:

A team project developed in early 2019 for the Michigan Tech sponsored hackathon winter wonder-hack where we won multiple prizes. It allows for android phones to connect to each other in a sort of network and infroms those closest to you of your approximate position and wether you are in danger during a disaster.

Implementation:

The implementation is fairly simple our team developed a small app in Java utilizing the toolkit provided by Android Studio. This app offered the user the ability to tell others if they were in danger and also to recieve signals from those in danger. This peer to peer signal transfereance was acomplished by integrating Google Nearby, a now deprecated API, the app enables connectivity between Android phones through various mediums such as Bluetooth, WiFi, and Data.

Technologies:

Java, Android Studio, Google Nearby
